E-waste, or electronic waste, includes discarded electronic devices like old phones, laptops, batteries, and home appliances. These items contain hazardous materials like lead and mercury, which can harm the environment if not disposed of properly. On the other hand, they also contain valuable materials like gold and copper that can be recycled. Improper disposal can lead to soil and water contamination, making responsible e-waste management crucial for a sustainable future.

It’s highly discouraged to dispose of electronics with regular trash because they often contain toxic substances that can leak into the ground and water supply. In many places, improper e-waste disposal is even illegal. When you toss electronics in the trash, they end up in landfills, where they release harmful chemicals over time. Recycling ensures that useful materials are recovered and reduces the need for new raw materials.
